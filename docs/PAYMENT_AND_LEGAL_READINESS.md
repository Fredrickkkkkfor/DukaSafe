# Payment and Legal Readiness

Last updated: 2026-07-01

## Current MVP Positioning

DukaSafe is currently positioned as a verified checkout and evidence-recording platform for Kenyan social commerce.

The MVP records:

- order terms;
- buyer details;
- seller trust/profile information;
- manual M-PESA payment proof;
- delivery proof;
- dispute evidence;
- admin decisions and audit logs.

## Funds Handling

The current MVP should be described as manual M-PESA proof tracking unless a legally approved fund-holding process exists.

Current assumed model:

- Buyer pays through seller-provided or DukaSafe-recorded M-PESA instructions.
- DukaSafe records payment proof and order terms.
- Seller dispatches after reviewing/accepting proof.
- Refunds are operational/legal processes outside automatic fund custody unless explicitly implemented and approved.

## Language Guidance

Do not use fund-custody or fund-holding language in production copy unless legal/operations approval exists.

Avoid:

- "escrow"
- "we hold your money"
- "guaranteed refund"
- "insured purchase"

Prefer:

- "protected checkout"
- "recorded order terms"
- "payment proof captured"
- "evidence-led dispute review"
- "buyer and seller protection rules"

## Buyer Protection Means

- Buyer can verify seller status before paying.
- Order terms are recorded before dispatch.
- Payment proof is stored for review.
- Delivery and dispute evidence can be reviewed by admin.
- DukaSafe provides structured dispute handling, not an unconditional refund guarantee.

## Seller Protection Means

- Seller receives recorded order terms.
- Seller can upload dispatch/delivery proof.
- Seller can respond to disputes.
- Admin review should consider both sides.
- False buyer claims can be reviewed against evidence.

## What DukaSafe Cannot Guarantee Without Further Approval

- Guaranteed refunds.
- Automatic recovery of funds sent outside the platform rules.
- Authenticity of every product beyond evidence review.
- Legal custody of funds.
- Chargeback-like reversal unless payment operations support it.

## Launch Requirement

Before public launch, a responsible operator should approve:

- public buyer protection wording;
- refund policy;
- seller terms;
- dispute timeline;
- who has refund authority;
- customer support escalation process.
