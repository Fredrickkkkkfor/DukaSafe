# DukaSafe Operations SOP

Last updated: 2026-07-01

## Seller Verification Rules

- Review identity, shop proof, social account ownership, and M-PESA/Till/Paybill details before approval.
- Do not approve a seller when ID proof is unreadable, social account ownership is unclear, or payment details conflict with the applicant.
- Use "Request More Info" when the application appears legitimate but incomplete.
- Use rejection only for ineligible, unverifiable, or unsafe applications.

## Document Review Checklist

- ID/passport is readable.
- Name is consistent with profile/application details.
- Document image is not obviously edited or reused.
- Shop photos match the claimed category.
- Private documents are viewed through signed/admin-only access only.

## Social Account Review Checklist

- TikTok/Instagram/WhatsApp links belong to the applicant's shop.
- Shop name and product category match the application.
- Social activity is plausible for Kenyan social commerce.
- Suspicious impersonation or copied account signals are flagged for manual review.

## M-PESA Proof Review Rules

- Confirm amount expected vs amount submitted.
- Confirm buyer phone/order details where available.
- Do not mark payment verified based only on a buyer statement.
- If proof is unclear, request review instead of confirming.

## Dispatch Proof Review Rules

- Seller should upload delivery proof after payment is accepted.
- Proof should include rider/courier, tracking note, receipt, photo, or delivery confirmation where available.
- Missing dispatch proof is a risk signal in disputes.

## Dispute Resolution Rules

- Review both buyer and seller evidence neutrally.
- Compare order terms, payment proof, delivery proof, messages/notes, and timeline.
- Require an admin note for every resolution.
- Use "More Evidence Needed" when the timeline or files are inconclusive.

## Outcome Criteria

- Refund: evidence supports buyer claim and seller failed to dispatch or materially breached terms.
- Partial refund: item or delivery issue is partly proven but not full non-delivery.
- Dismissed: seller evidence and timeline support seller fulfillment.
- More evidence needed: key proof is missing or unclear.

## Seller Suspension Criteria

- Repeated unresolved disputes.
- Evidence of forged documents or payment details.
- Seller disappears after payment proof.
- High-risk reports with supporting evidence.
- Serious breach during dispute review.

## Report Triage Process

- Treat reports as private operational signals, not public blacklists.
- Link report to seller/order when possible.
- Add an admin note before dismissing or escalating.
- Suspend only when evidence and risk justify it.

## Admin Audit Expectations

- Every approve/reject/request-info action should create an audit log.
- Every dispute resolution should create an audit log and timeline event.
- Every suspension/reactivation should create an audit log.
- Admin notes should be factual, concise, and evidence-based.

## Response Timelines

- Seller verification target: 24-48 hours.
- Seller dispute response target: 24 hours unless policy states otherwise.
- Admin dispute review target: under 72 hours after sufficient evidence is available.
- Urgent risk/suspension review: same day.
